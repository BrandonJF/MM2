American Medical Technologists (AMT) is a private, non-profit certifying organization. Certification by AMT as a Registered Dental Assistant, RDA (AMT), indicates that an individual meets certain educational and/or experience requirements established by AMT and has passed AMT’s written examination. The RDA (AMT) certification does NOT, however, indicate that the individual has complied with any licensure, registration, or other regulatory requirements of the state(s) in which he or she intends to practice. A number of states administer licensure and/or registration programs for dental assistants. Some states’ programs are mandatory for all dental assistants practicing within the state; others are voluntary for those dental assistants who wish to perform expanded duties. Certification by AMT does NOT take the place of any state program, mandatory or voluntary. In addition to obtaining the RDA (AMT) credential, you should contact your state’s dental licensing board to determine whether you are in compliance with any state laws and regulations governing the practice of dental assisting.
